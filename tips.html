Security Tips

Good password habits make your accounts much harder to attack. Use these practical tips to stay safer online.

Use a Password Manager

A password manager helps you generate, store, and autofill unique passwords for every site so you never reuse passwords.

Enable Two-Factor Authentication

Whenever possible, enable 2FA for your accounts. This adds a second layer of security beyond your password.

Look Out for Phishing

Never enter passwords from links in unsolicited emails or messages. Always navigate to sites directly via trusted bookmarks.

Know When to Change Passwords

Change passwords if a service reports a breach, you reuse one across multiple sites, or you suspect it may be compromised.

Check for Data Breaches

Regularly verify if your email or phone number has been exposed in past data leaks using trusted breach-notification services.

Keep Software Up-to-Date

Enable automatic updates for your OS and apps. Security patches are your first line of defense against newly discovered vulnerabilities.
